# Synthetic Content Disclosure Policy

## Purpose

Define when synthetic or AI-assisted content disclosure is mandatory and how absence of required disclosure blocks publishing.

## When Disclosure Is Mandatory

Disclosure is mandatory when any of the following apply:

- Content is fully generated by AI.
- Content is materially transformed by AI (rewriting, voice cloning, image/video synthesis, or significant compositing).
- AI materially contributes to claims, recommendations, or narrative framing presented to end users.
- Destination platform, partner policy, or local policy requires synthetic content labeling.

## Minimum Disclosure Standard

A valid disclosure must be:

- **Clear**: understandable to a reasonable user.
- **Conspicuous**: easy to find before or at first meaningful consumption.
- **Specific**: indicates AI/synthetic assistance rather than vague “enhanced” wording.
- **Persistent**: remains attached where required (e.g., metadata label + visible notice).

## Invalid Disclosure Patterns

- Hidden, collapsed, or low-contrast labels.
- Ambiguous wording (“optimized,” “smartly produced”) without explicit AI mention.
- Disclosures shown only after user action when policy requires upfront notice.
- Batch-level disclosure used for individual assets when per-asset labeling is required.

## Enforcement and Publishing Gate

- If disclosure is required and absent or invalid, decision is **BLOCK**.
- BLOCK remains until disclosure is corrected and re-validated.
- Manual override cannot bypass mandatory disclosure requirements.
- Repeated disclosure failures should trigger escalated monitoring and workflow remediation.

## Review Artifacts

Store the following for auditability:

- Disclosure text and placement evidence (screenshot or metadata snapshot).
- Policy basis that triggered mandatory disclosure.
- Validation result and timestamp.
